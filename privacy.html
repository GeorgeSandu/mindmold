How OneRoom Started

OneRoom started off as a group of friends brainstorming about a video game. They wanted a game that was immersive and productive at the same time. The development started in September 2015, in Dimitri Papigiotis's bedroom where George Sandu, Dimitri Papigiotis, and Jonathan Cali would work on game development. Later on Sanito Ricatto joined to help with development. Since the idea was finished, the first thing we had to do was to design the maps and music.

 

Building Blocks To Success

Designing the maps was the core of development, because it's what emphasizes the purpose of the game being Memorization and Place Learning. "Place Learning" is a method that exercises the brain's memorization tactics. This subconsciously trains the player for real life scenarios such as remembering directions and streets. Memorization is a key factor of responsibility, and MindMold is a game that can train respectable traits as such.

 

Implementation

The MindMold team used strategical thinking learned through internet research on place learning to implement map designs. A splash screen showing players what they have to memorize is implemented for six or ten seconds. Falling tiles, pathways, and restarting on death have also been recently implemented . Now the MindMold team makes more levels to focus on memorization as well as hand eye coordination. Some more features implemented in Mindmold are the turrets and black bars in the level to make the game have an obstacle course feel.

 

Extra Creations

Music was added throughout the game, made using Logic Pro X for its production. MindMold also has a promotional website made which highlights the key points of OneRoom's mission and also has a suggestions page. Another idea that was implemented in MindMold was the black bars that were added to the game. The idea of the black bars were to add challenge to the player, and add hand eye coordination training as well as practicing memorization.

 

About Us

MindMold's company, OneRoom, is a group of four high school students working to develop a new kind of game that helps to excersize people's minds.
